// 01 — THE PROBLEM

Studying lost the war for attention.

A college student will grind 40 hours into an RPG in a week and then can't open a textbook for 20 minutes. The dopamine math is brutal — games reward you every ten seconds, lectures reward you in four months.

The "study apps" trying to fix this — flashcards, timers, streak trackers — feel like chores wearing a productivity costume. None of them compete with what a 19-year-old's brain actually wants to do at 11pm on a Tuesday.

Educavia does — because Educavia is the game they want to be playing.

// 02 — THE SOLUTION

Real study unlocks real game power.

Educavia flips the model. The game is the prize — a cinematic 3D RPG with custom characters, real combat, multiple themed universes, and worlds worth exploring. Real studying is the only path to progression.

To upgrade weapons, unlock animations, evolve your character, or open new zones — you have to learn. An Expert AI cross-checks your stated goals against what you actually studied, so the system can't be gamed. Timed study sessions track effort. In-game questions provide secondary leveling.

Real learning translates directly into real game power.

// 03 — WHY NOW

Three things just became true.

01

AI Can Finally Verify Learning

Not just "did the user click" — but whether they actually engaged with what they said they would study. That changes the entire incentive structure.

02

One Dev Can Ship a World

Modern engines let small teams build experiences that used to require AAA studios with hundreds of people and tens of millions of dollars.

03

Attention Migrated

Gen Z's attention has fully moved from textbooks to games. Meeting them where they are is no longer a nice-to-have — it's the only path forward.
